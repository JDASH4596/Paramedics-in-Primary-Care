Purpose

This website is designed as a quick-reference clinical support tool to assist clinicians working in same-day, sudden and serious presentations, particularly within primary care and urgent care settings.

What This Tool Is Designed to Do

  • Support rapid assessment and decision-making
  • Highlight key red flags, differentials, and early management considerations
  • Provide structured prompts to aid safe clinical reasoning

Important Disclaimer

  • Does not replace clinical judgement
  • Does not replace current national or local guidelines
  • Does not replace best practice, training, or senior clinical advice

All clinical decisions must be made using your professional judgement, the individual patient context, and the most up-to-date national and local guidance (e.g. NICE, service protocols).

Use in Clinical Practice

  • A supporting reference — not a definitive guide
  • A prompt for consideration — not a rigid checklist
  • A starting point — not the end of assessment

If there is uncertainty, deterioration, or concern: err on the side of safety, escalate appropriately, and follow local emergency and safeguarding pathways.

Clinical Responsibility

Responsibility for patient care always remains with the clinician using this tool. The creators accept no liability for clinical decisions made without appropriate judgement, guideline consultation, or escalation.

Keeping Practice Current

Clinical guidance evolves over time. This tool should always be used alongside current evidence, updated guidelines, local protocols, and ongoing professional development.

Clinical support only. This site is a quick reference tool and does not replace clinical judgement or current guidelines.
