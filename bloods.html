Blood Test Interpretation

Foundations for new clinicians in primary care

How to Think About Blood Tests

  • Use bloods to answer: unsafe now? fits presentation? repeat vs investigate?
  • Always check degree of abnormality and trend.
  • One abnormal result alone rarely gives a diagnosis.

Clinical Interpretation Loop (KISS)

  • Context: why was it taken? symptoms? meds? hydration? alcohol?
  • Pattern: interpret as systems (FBC / U&E / LFT pattern).
  • Trend: new vs chronic; rising vs stable.
  • Action: repeat, investigate, treat, refer, or urgent escalation.

Common Primary Care Pitfalls

  • Overreacting to mild isolated abnormalities without a plan to repeat/confirm.
  • Ignoring hydration, intercurrent illness, and medication effects.
  • Not checking historical baselines (AKI vs CKD; chronic LFT rise, etc.).
