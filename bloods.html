System-Based Interpretation

  • FBC: anaemia, infection, marrow response
  • U&E: renal function and volume status
  • LFTs: hepatocellular vs cholestatic pattern
  • CRP: presence of active inflammation
  • HbA1c: long-term glucose control

Degree of Abnormality

  • Mild abnormalities often require monitoring.
  • Moderate abnormalities require investigation.
  • Severe abnormalities require urgent action.

Magnitude guides urgency in primary care.

Trends Over Time

  • Compare with previous results.
  • New abnormalities matter more than chronic ones.
  • Rate of change is often more important than value.

Common Pitfalls

  • Treating numbers instead of patients.
  • Over-investigating mild abnormalities.
  • Ignoring medication effects.

Primary Care Decision Loop

  • Is this immediately unsafe?
  • Does it explain the presentation?
  • Does it need repeating?
  • Does it need investigation or referral?
