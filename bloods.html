Blood Test Interpretation

Foundations for new clinicians in primary care

Clinical Interpretation Loop (KISS)

  • Context: why was it taken? symptoms? meds? hydration? alcohol?
  • Pattern: interpret as systems (FBC / U&E / LFT pattern).
  • Trend: new vs chronic; rising vs stable.
  • Action: repeat, investigate, treat, refer, or urgent escalation.

Blood tests refine probability — they rarely create diagnoses.

When you would need to request for (Add-on tests)

Indication guide for new clinicians. Align with local pathways and patient context.

Ferritin / Iron Studies

Request when

Low Hb and/or low MCV • fatigue with risk factors • suspected blood loss • thrombocytosis with unclear cause

Interpretation tip

Ferritin can be falsely normal/high in inflammation — consider CRP and full iron profile if unclear.

B12 / Folate

Request when

Raised MCV • neuro symptoms (paraesthesia/gait) • glossitis/mouth ulcers • dietary/malabsorption risk • metformin/PPIs long-term

Safety note

If neuro symptoms + suspected B12 deficiency, act promptly per local guidance while confirming.

HbA1c

Request when

Symptoms suggestive of hyperglycaemia • incidental raised glucose • diabetes risk factors • monitoring known diabetes/prediabetes

Use alongside

Consider fasting glucose if you need a “right now” answer (acute symptoms/unwell).

Other common add-ons (quick triggers)

TFTs

Fatigue • weight change • palpitations • temperature intolerance • menstrual change • AF assessment

Lipids

CV risk assessment • baseline before statin • metabolic syndrome

Urine ACR

Diabetes • hypertension • CKD monitoring • unexplained oedema

Coeliac screen

Iron deficiency • chronic GI symptoms • weight loss • family history (ensure gluten intake)

Common Primary Care Pitfalls

  • Ordering broad panels without a clinical question (noise → over-investigation).
  • Reacting to mild isolated abnormalities without repeat/confirmation.
  • Ignoring hydration, illness, and medication effects.
  • Not checking historical baseline (AKI vs CKD; chronic LFT rise, etc.).
