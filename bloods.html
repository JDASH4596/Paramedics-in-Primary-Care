Blood Test Interpretation

Foundations for new clinicians in primary care

Clinical Interpretation Loop (KISS)

  • Context: why was it taken? symptoms? meds? hydration? alcohol?
  • Pattern: interpret as systems (FBC / U&E / LFT pattern).
  • Trend: new vs chronic; rising vs stable.
  • Action: repeat, investigate, treat, refer, or urgent escalation.

Blood tests refine probability — they rarely create diagnoses.

Targets vary by cardiovascular risk and local guidance. Treat as a risk tool, not a standalone diagnosis.

Lipids: When to order / repeat / escalate

Keep aligned to local guidance. This is a practical primary care approach for new clinicians.

When to order lipids

CV risk assessment (e.g., QRISK) • diabetes • hypertension • CKD • NAFLD/metabolic syndrome • before starting statin • strong family history / premature CVD

Fasting vs non-fasting

Non-fasting is often acceptable for risk scoring. Consider fasting if triglycerides are raised, suspected familial dyslipidaemia, or for follow-up clarity.

When to repeat

After lifestyle advice (typically weeks–months) • after starting/changing statin (per local pathway) • if results likely affected by acute illness

When to suspect Familial Hypercholesterolaemia (FH)

Markedly raised cholesterol/LDL • premature CVD in patient or 1st-degree relatives • tendon xanthomas / corneal arcus (young). Use local FH pathway.

Escalate / act urgently

Very high triglycerides (pancreatitis risk) • symptomatic CVD concerns • results suggesting FH in line with local criteria

When you would need to request for (Add-on tests)

Indication guide for new clinicians. Align with local pathways and patient context.

Ferritin / Iron Studies

Request when

Low Hb and/or low MCV • fatigue with risk factors • suspected blood loss • thrombocytosis with unclear cause

Interpretation tip

Ferritin can be falsely normal/high in inflammation — consider CRP and full iron profile if unclear.

B12 / Folate

Request when

Raised MCV • neuro symptoms • glossitis/mouth ulcers • dietary/malabsorption risk • metformin/PPIs long-term

Safety note

If neuro symptoms + suspected B12 deficiency, act promptly per local guidance while confirming.

HbA1c

Request when

Symptoms suggestive of hyperglycaemia • incidental raised glucose • diabetes risk factors • monitoring diabetes/prediabetes

Use alongside

Consider fasting glucose if you need a “right now” answer (acute symptoms/unwell).

Other common add-ons (quick triggers)

TFTs

Fatigue • weight change • palpitations • temperature intolerance • menstrual change • AF assessment

Urine ACR

Diabetes • hypertension • CKD monitoring • unexplained oedema

Coeliac screen

Iron deficiency • chronic GI symptoms • weight loss • family history (ensure gluten intake)

Vitamin D

Bone pain/fractures • falls risk • limited sun exposure • malabsorption (local testing guidance varies)

Common Primary Care Pitfalls

  • Ordering broad panels without a clinical question (noise → over-investigation).
  • Reacting to mild isolated abnormalities without repeat/confirmation.
  • Ignoring hydration, illness, and medication effects.
  • Not checking historical baseline (AKI vs CKD; chronic LFT rise, etc.).
