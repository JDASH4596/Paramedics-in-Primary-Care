Blood Test Interpretation

Foundations for new clinicians in primary care

How to Think About Blood Tests

  • Blood tests support decisions — they rarely make diagnoses.
  • Always interpret results in clinical context.
  • Degree and trend matter more than isolated values.

Clinical Interpretation Framework

  • Why was the test requested?
  • Does the result explain the presentation?
  • Is this new, worsening, or long-standing?
  • Does this need repeating, investigating, or acting on?

Common Primary Care Pitfalls

  • Reacting to isolated mild abnormalities.
  • Treating numbers instead of patients.
  • Ignoring medication and hydration effects.
