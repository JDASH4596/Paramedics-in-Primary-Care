Abdominal

Common abdominal presentations with quick primary-care actions.

🍽️ Dyspepsia / GORD

Open guidance
  • Assess alarm symptoms: dysphagia, weight loss, bleeding.
  • Trial lifestyle + acid suppression where appropriate.
  • Review NSAID use, alcohol, smoking, diet triggers.
  • Refer urgently if red flags or persistent alarm features.

🦠 Gastroenteritis

Open guidance
  • Hydration status and severity first.
  • Usually self-limiting; oral rehydration advice.
  • Safety net for blood in stool, persistent fever, reduced urine.
  • Consider stool tests when prolonged/severe/outbreak risk.

⚠️ Appendicitis suspicion

Open guidance
  • Migratory pain to RIF, fever, anorexia, guarding concern.
  • Primary care role is early recognition and urgent surgical route.
  • Avoid delaying referral for serial primary care reviews if worsening.

🧱 Biliary colic / cholecystitis

Open guidance
  • RUQ pain after fatty meals ± nausea/vomiting.
  • Check fever/jaundice/systemic upset (possible complication).
  • Arrange LFT/CRP/FBC and pathway-based imaging/referral.

🩸 GI bleed concern

Open guidance
  • Melaena, haematemesis, or maroon stool = urgent pathway.
  • Assess haemodynamic stability and symptoms of shock.
  • Immediate escalation rather than delayed outpatient workup.

🚽 IBS / functional symptoms

Open guidance
  • Positive diagnosis approach + symptom pattern.
  • Exclude red flags and key differentials where indicated.
  • Dietary strategy, stress/sleep review, and follow-up plan.

Use local and NICE pathways for referral timing, safety thresholds, and treatment choices.
