Skin

Quick-reference dermatology conditions and red-flag prompts.

🧴 Eczema flare

Open guidance
  • Assess trigger pattern, infection signs, and severity.
  • Emollient optimisation + topical steroid ladder.
  • Check adherence and steroid-phobia barriers.
  • Escalate if extensive, infected, or poor control.

🔥 Cellulitis

Open guidance
  • Warmth, erythema, tenderness, spreading margin.
  • Mark edge, consider systemic symptoms and comorbidity.
  • Oral antibiotics per pathway if uncomplicated.
  • Urgent escalation if sepsis concern/rapid progression.

🌿 Contact dermatitis

Open guidance
  • Identify likely irritant/allergen exposure.
  • Avoidance strategy + barrier/skin-care advice.
  • Topical anti-inflammatory treatment when flared.
  • Consider patch-testing referral if recurrent/severe.

🫧 Urticaria

Open guidance
  • Transient itchy weals ± angioedema assessment.
  • Non-sedating antihistamine first-line.
  • Trigger review: infection, meds, foods, stress.
  • Emergency pathway for airway/breathing compromise.

🦠 Shingles

Open guidance
  • Dermatomal painful vesicular rash, unilateral pattern.
  • Antiviral window and pain management review.
  • Eye involvement is urgent same-day ophthalmology route.

⚠️ Suspicious lesion

Open guidance
  • Use ABCDE/ugly duckling checks and change over time.
  • Document size, border, colour, symptoms, photo if policy allows.
  • Urgent suspected-cancer pathway when criteria met.

Follow local/NICE skin and urgent referral pathways for diagnosis and management thresholds.
